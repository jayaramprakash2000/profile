About Me

I am a passionate researcher specializing in fractional differential equations, mathematical modeling, and applied analysis. Currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Mathematics, my work focuses on developing novel semi-analytical techniques and integral transform-based methods to solve real-world problems arising in science and engineering.

With a strong foundation in theoretical mathematics and practical computation, I strive to bridge the gap between abstract mathematical frameworks and their impactful applications. My research journey includes the modeling and simulation of epidemic outbreaks and the development of fractional-order control strategies.

In addition to research, I am actively involved in academic collaborations and conference presentations in mathematical modeling. I believe in the power of mathematics not only as a tool for problem-solving but as a lens to view, understand, and transform the world around us.
